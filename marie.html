MARIE Sim

A MARIE Simulator and Assembly Language

in C++

MARIE Sim is an implementation of a simulator of the MARIE architecture

MARIE stands for Machine Architecture that is Really Intuitive and Easy

It's a theoretical CPU architecture designed for educational environments

MARIE Sim was coded in C++ and consists of two parts:

the SIMULATOR that runs MARIE Programs

the ASEEMBLER that allows the user to program for MARIE in a language similar to IBM Assembler

Reflection

Repo available: Click Here

As part of my Computer Systems and Architecture class at Northern Illinois University, I explored the MARIE CPU architecture. To deepen my understanding (and to hopefully assist with my homework!) I decided to create a simulator for it. What started as a simple project quickly evolved into a full assembler and simple assembly language.

My assembly language for this project is inspired by IBM Assembler but features stricter syntax, enhancing clarity and usability for the unique architecture. This project provided me with invaluable insights into how computers operate at the most fundamental level.

One challenge I tackled with my newly developed assembly language was implementing integer division in the MARIE architecture. Although it was a complex and somewhat tedious task, successfully achieving this functionality reinforced my belief that if something is possible, it's worth pursuing.

Overall, developing the MARIE simulator and assembler has greatly enhanced my understanding of computer architecture at the lower levels, making it a rewarding experience in my academic journey.
